Tuesday, a little after midnight —
My Eloise,

There is a dress you have not worn since the night we met — the one with the thin straps, the one your mother called too forward. Put it on for me.

Don't do your hair. Don't ask me why. Leave the top two buttons open on your coat when you step out into the cold, and walk slowly — slowly enough that I could find you if I came looking.

I want to see how you carry it now, after everything we have been.

Where will you go?

i.
Dinner at Leonie's. Where he first saw you. The waiter still remembers your face, and the dress, and the way you laughed when the candle went out.
ii.
A walk by the river, alone. Stop at the bridge. Send one photograph, just one, with no caption — he will know exactly what it means.
iii.
Stay in. Pour something dark. Sit by the window. Leave the curtain half-drawn. Wait for him to think of you — he will, he always does.
